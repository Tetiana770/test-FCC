The history of the development of artificial intelligence

Birth (1950-1956)

In the first half of the 20th century, science fiction introduced the world to the concept of robots with artificial intelligence.The history of artificial intelligence dates back to the 1950s, and by then the concept of artificial intelligence had been assimilated by enthusiasts of that period.

One such person was Alan Turing, a young British scholar who explored the mathematical possibilities of artificial intelligence. Turing suggested that humans use reason to solve problems and make decisions, so why shouldn't machines do the same? This idea formed the basis of his 1950 paper Computing and Intelligence , in which he described how to create intelligent machines and how to test their intelligence.

  • 1955 - Allen Newell and Herbert Simon created the first artificial intelligence program - Logic Theorist. She proved 38 out of 52 mathematical theorems and also found new proofs for some others.

  • 1956 - the term "artificial intelligence" was first used by the American computer scientist John McCarthy at the Dartmouth conference.

Golden years (1956-1974)

  • 1966 - Joseph Weitzenbaum developed the first chatbot - ELIZA. It was named after Eliza Doolittle, the heroine of Bernard Shaw's play "Pygmalion", who was taught the language of the "higher class of people". ELIZA simulated a dialogue with a psychotherapist.

  • 1972 ― the first intelligent humanoid robot, named WABOT-1, was created in Japan.

The first "winter of artificial intelligence" (1974-1980)

During this period, computer scientists faced an acute shortage of funding.

AI Boom (1980-1987)

In 1980, the first national conference of the American Association for Artificial Intelligence was held at Stanford University.

The second "AI winter" (1987-1993)

Investors and governments have once again stopped funding research in the field of artificial intelligence due to high costs and inefficient results.

The emergence of intelligent agents (1993-2011)

  • 1997 - the IBM Deep Blue computer beat the world chess champion Garry Kasparov.

  • 2002 - AI entered the home for the first time in the form of the Roomba vacuum cleaner.

  • 2006 - Businesses began using the technology, including Facebook, Twitter and Netflix.

Deep learning, Big Data and artificial general intelligence (2011-present)

  • 2011 - IBM's Watson won Jeopardy, where it had to solve complex riddles. It became clear: he understands natural language, quickly solves difficult questions.

  • 2012 ― Google launched the Google Now feature in the Android app, which could provide the user with information in the form of a forecast.

  • 2020 ― Baidu released the LinearFold AI algorithm for medical and research teams developing a vaccine in the early stages of the SARS-CoV-2 (COVID-19) pandemic.

The algorithm can predict the RNA sequence of the virus in just 27 seconds. It is 120 times faster than other methods.

Today, artificial intelligence is one of the most popular terms in the world. According to some forecasts, AI will bring $15.7 trillion to the global economy by 2035. He already creates a lot of digital content - texts, pictures, music, videos, etc. The basics of its functioning should be known at least in order not to lose business or work.
